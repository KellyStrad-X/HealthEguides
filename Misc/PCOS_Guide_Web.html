Understanding PCOS

What PCOS actually is, how it's diagnosed, and the four key mechanisms affecting your body

What PCOS Actually Is

"Polycystic Ovary Syndrome" is misleading. PCOS is a complex hormonal and metabolic disorder affecting insulin, androgens, inflammation, and reproductive function.

The 4 Key Mechanisms

üç¨ Insulin Resistance

Affects 70% of PCOS patients. Drives many symptoms.

üìà Elevated Androgens

High testosterone causes acne, excess hair, hair loss.

üî• Inflammation

Low-grade inflammation worsens insulin resistance.

ü•ö Irregular Ovulation

Unpredictable cycles and fertility issues.

How PCOS Is Diagnosed

Rotterdam Criteria ‚Äî You need 2 out of 3:

1. Irregular/Absent Ovulation
Unpredictable cycles, missed periods, long cycles (35+ days)
2. Elevated Androgens
Acne, excess hair, hair loss, OR high testosterone on blood tests
3. Polycystic Ovaries
Multiple small follicles on ultrasound

The Fertility Factor

PCOS is the leading cause of female infertility ‚Äî but most women can conceive with treatment

Fertility with PCOS

You're not infertile. You're ovulating irregularly, and that's treatable. The majority of women with PCOS can conceive with appropriate treatment.

Preconception Optimization

Lifestyle optimization: 3-6 months of nutrition, exercise, supplements improve egg quality
Start inositol: Improves egg quality and ovulation rates
Track cycles: Apps, basal body temp, ovulation kits
See doctor early: Don't wait 12 months‚Äîseek help after 6 months
Test partner's sperm: 30% of infertility has male factor

Working with Your Doctor

How to ensure proper testing, advocate for yourself, and find knowledgeable providers

How to Work with Your Doctor

PCOS diagnosis and treatment vary wildly across providers. Here's how to ensure proper care.

Diagnostic Tests to Request

Hormone Panel: Total/free testosterone, DHEA-S, LH, FSH, prolactin
Metabolic Panel: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, lipid panel
Thyroid Panel: TSH, free T4, free T3 (rule out thyroid issues)
Ultrasound: Transvaginal to assess ovaries

üí° Important to Know

PCOS is a clinical diagnosis‚Äîtests support it but aren't definitive. Diagnosis based on symptoms + hormone patterns + ultrasound (Rotterdam criteria: need 2 of 3).

Conversation Scripts

"I have symptoms consistent with PCOS including [list top 3]. Can we run comprehensive hormone and metabolic testing?"

"I'd like to discuss PCOS treatment options including metformin, lifestyle interventions, and emerging approaches like GLP-1 medications."

"Can you refer me to an endocrinologist or reproductive endocrinologist who specializes in PCOS?"

üö© Red Flags: When to Find a New Provider

Consider finding new doctor if they:

  • Dismiss symptoms as "just lose weight" without addressing how or offering support
  • Refuse diagnostic tests without good reason
  • Only offer birth control without discussing other options
  • Aren't familiar with current PCOS research or treatment approaches
  • Don't listen or repeatedly cut you off during appointments
  • Make you feel dismissed, judged, or not taken seriously
